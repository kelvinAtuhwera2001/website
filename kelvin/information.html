Environmental Information Center

Knowledge is power when it comes to environmental conservation. Below you'll find comprehensive information about key environmental issues and what you can do to help.

Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns, primarily caused by human activities like burning fossil fuels.

Key Facts:

  • Global temperatures have risen about 1.1Â°C since the pre-industrial era
  • The last decade (2011-2020) was the warmest on record
  • Sea levels are rising about 3.7mm per year
  • Extreme weather events are becoming more frequent and intense

What You Can Do:

  • Reduce energy consumption at home
  • Switch to renewable energy sources
  • Use public transport, bike, or walk when possible
  • Support policies and businesses that prioritize sustainability

Deforestation

Deforestation is the large-scale removal of trees from forests for agriculture, logging, or development, leading to habitat loss and increased CO2 levels.

Key Facts:

  • 10 million hectares of forest are lost each year
  • Forests are home to 80% of terrestrial biodiversity
  • Deforestation accounts for about 10% of global greenhouse gas emissions
  • An estimated 15 billion trees are cut down each year

What You Can Do:

  • Choose sustainable wood and paper products
  • Support reforestation projects
  • Reduce consumption of products linked to deforestation (palm oil, beef, etc.)
  • Eat a more plant-based diet

Plastic Pollution

Plastic pollution is the accumulation of plastic objects in the environment that adversely affects wildlife, habitats, and humans.

Key Facts:

  • Over 300 million tons of plastic are produced annually
  • About 8 million tons of plastic end up in oceans each year
  • Plastic takes 400+ years to degrade
  • 90% of seabirds have plastic in their stomachs

What You Can Do:

  • Use reusable bags, bottles, and containers
  • Avoid single-use plastics
  • Participate in local cleanups
  • Recycle properly and support plastic reduction policies
